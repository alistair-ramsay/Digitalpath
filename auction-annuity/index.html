AuctionMyAnnuity is looking to develop a service for annuity holders to compare offers from several providers and sell their annuity to the highest bidder for a cash lump sum.

In the 2015 pre-election budget the government announced a consultation on allowing holders of annuities to sell their annuity income to release the value. This has become known as the Secondary Annuity Market and is expected to begin in 2017.

AuctionMyAnnuity is working with both annuity providers and advisers to build a service that should help customers obtain the best offer for the sale of their annuity income. Rules relating to this market are yet to be confirmed by the FCA but are expected in 2017.

AuctionMyAnnuity's proposal will be a service available for use either directly by annuity holders who are receiving a pension income, or by an adviser/family member on their behalf. The service should allow for comparison of offers and sale of the annuity to the highest bidder.

This is how we think it would work...

  1. Enter your details

    Firstly, you would need you to complete your personal information and give us any detail relating to the annuity you wish to sell. We would the need you to complete the medical history and lifestyle information for both yourself and any dependant who would receive an income from the annuity after your death. At this point, we would outline to you the expected costs associated with selling your annuity.

  2. Personalised illustration

    Next we would provide you with a Personalised Illustration (non-guaranteed) to show how much you might receive. This would be non-binding and based only on the information you entered. We would then provide a comparison illustration showing the cost of buying a new annuity to replace your income on the open market.

  3. Confirm your identity

    If you wish to proceed, and get a binding offer from the buyers on our panel, we would then carry out an ID check to confirm your identity. We would also check with your provider to confirm the full details of your annuity and to ensure they allow it to be sold (re-assigned).

  4. Listing fee

    You would then be charged a listing fee to cover some of the costs of medical interview we would need to conduct and also to cover the costs of administering the annuity auction.

  5. Your medical

    You (and any dependants on your annuity) would then be interviewed on the telephone by a nurse to assess your health and perform an assessment of your vulnerability, a General Practitioner's Report (GPR) may also be requested from your doctor. We anticipate this process would take between two and four weeks but it may take longer.

  6. Potential buyers advised

    The confirmed medical details and annuity policy details would then be sent to each potential buyer in the market, so they can make an offer if they wish.

  7. Your annuity auction

    Your annuity auction would then remain live for 7 days, during which period buyers can make their offers. At the end of this period you would then be able to review any offers made and choose if you would like to proceed with one of them.

  8. Sign the paperwork

    If you wish to proceed, we would then notify your chosen buyer that you wish to proceed. You would then need to sign and return some paper work. NOTE: If your annuity is above a certain level you may need to confirm and prove that you have received advice from a qualified financial adviser. If this has not been done, you would then need to arrange for such advice before you can proceed.

  9. Confirm you wish to sell

    Once you confirm your wish to sell, you would then enter a 14 day cooling off period during which time the process would pause to allow you time to re-consider this important decision. During this period, you have the mandatory right to change your mind. After this period, the buyer would then make arrangements with the assigning annuity provider to transfer the annuity income to them. Once this has happened the buyer would pay you your cash lump sum. NOTE: The cash lump sum would be subject to income tax at your marginal rate. The tax would be deducted by the buyer prior to you receiving the money, in the same way as currently occurs with your annuity income. You may have further tax liability dependent on your personal taxable income position.
